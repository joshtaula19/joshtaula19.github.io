Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

Trying to think back to a time where I made an ethical decision is a difficult task, although one thing that comes to mind is a decision I had to make when I was 13 years old in school. One of my friends had lied to our teacher last minute to get out of taking a test, knowing that I, at the time, had also not prepared well enough for this test was unhappy that they got away with lying about being sick in order to get more time to study for the test and eventually take it at a later date. Knowing that this was an end of year exam and it put my friend at an advantage to the rest of my class, I considered telling on him as I thought it was unfair and almost deemed it as cheating in order to score better on this test. The ethical decision at hand was whether I valued this friendship more or staying academically integeral. The decision I chose was to not tell on my friend. Weighing up the options, I realised that I valued my friendship and the trust they put in me more than a test in school. Although I definitely dont condone or reccomend cheating in any way. I am grateful that I made this decision as now looking back on it I can't even remember what the test was about and I am still good friends with that person to this day. I definitely in no way regret the decision I made and I think that I value loyalty and trust in my everyday life over a middle school test.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

One time when I was trying to work productively with others was while I was studying at the University Of Canterbury, doing a degree in music, I was put into a group and was tasked with creating a song together. Each group was assigned one 3rd year student and the rest being 1st and 2nd year students. As the 3rd year student I was designated the leader and was in charge of bringing together the group as well as making the final decisions on what song we make. Due to this being a Music course, students who enjoyed all types of different genres were involved. This caused a lot of problems as some group members didn't like or want to create songs in a certain genre that others may have wanted to. Being the group leader it was my job to make the final decision. I felt a lot of pressure within this role because I was unsure of the best way to go about it. We ended up doing a group vote and going with the majority decision. Which in turn left at least one group member unhappy. I think that although we got the assignment in and got a good grade on it, I probably would go a different route about a situation like that now looking back on it. I think that something like asking everyone to create a list of 10 genres they enjoy and trying to find common ground as oppossed to a majority decision might work better. Or even trying a genre which no one was familiar with in order to keep everyone at a level playing field and that way no one gets their way and everyone is trying something fun and new.
