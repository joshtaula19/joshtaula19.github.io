How to get out of a sticky situation

Throughout this most recent sprint (sprint 5) I ran into so many issues, some bigger, and some smaller. A lot of the time it felt easier to just give up, also felt pretty out of my depth a lot of the time. One of the main things was just to keep going. One big problem I ran into, and have struggled with in the past, is understanding loops. A concept which i've struggled with over and over. This time was a little different from the last as I had learnt and put into practice different problem solving techniques. One of the main techniques which helped me was asking peers of help. I was fortunate enough to have friends which spent a lot of time at university studying computer science and they were able to help me when I was stuck. One thing which I found quite difficult in this was asking for help in it self. It can be intimidating as well as feel silly to ask simple questions. Once I was able to tackle that hurdle it helped me get to where I needed to be.

Eleganty Solved a Problem

One of many issues I ran into was around math calculations as well as simple typos in my code, a lot of the time it would cause headaches and anger as I was unable to figure out what was wrong. I realised that sitting at the computer for such a long time was only making things worse. One thing that benefited me greatly was as simple as taking a break for a little bit. Getting a cup of tea/coffee and just taking some time away from the screen to reset my brain a little bit. When I was able to come back to it I came in with a clear head and was able to figure out what was missing. Making sure I wasn't rushing or pushing myself too much helped me to understand where I was going wrong.
